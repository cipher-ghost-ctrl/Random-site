What It Packs

Boardroom specs. Basement prices.

A machine originally sold to enterprise clients for four figures is now a hobbyist's dream. Here's why the 7050 SFF punches so far above its price.

🧠
7th Gen Intel Core

Support up to the i7-7700 — 4 cores, 8 threads. Quick-sync hardware encode, AES-NI encryption, and enough grunt for virtualization, compiling, and light gaming simultaneously.

i3 / i5 / i7 4 Cores · 8 Threads 65W TDP
💾
Expandable RAM · Up to 64GB

Four DIMM slots running dual-channel DDR4 at 2400 MT/s. Most units ship with 8–16GB — drop in two 32GB sticks and turn this into a workstation that'd embarrass machines five times the price.

DDR4 · 2400 MT/s 4x DIMM Dual Channel
🎮
Low-Profile GPU Ready

The PCIe slot accepts low-profile cards natively. Slide in a GT 1050 Ti LP and you've got a 1080p gaming machine for the price of a takeaway. The chassis was never meant for this. That's the point.

PCIe x16 75W GPU Limit 1080p Gaming
⚡
Modern Storage · M.2 NVMe

An M.2 slot with NVMe support sits alongside a 3.5" SATA bay. Pair a 500GB NVMe boot drive with a 4TB spinning disk and you've got a homelab-ready storage configuration inside a box the size of a hardback book.

NVMe M.2 3.5" SATA Bay Dual Storage
🔌
Connectivity · USB-C & Beyond

Front and rear USB-A, a rear USB Type-C, dual DisplayPort 1.2 outputs for a twin-monitor setup, and legacy HDMI. Plus Gigabit Ethernet on the Intel I219-LM NIC — the one actually worth having.

USB-C 2× DisplayPort Intel I219-LM
🔒
Security · TPM 2.0 Hardened

TPM 2.0 onboard means Windows 11 is technically unlockable with a registry patch, BitLocker works natively, and secure boot is supported. Enterprise-grade hardware security for the price of a bus ticket.

TPM 2.0 Secure Boot BitLocker

Why SFF?

No bulk. Just capability.

The cult following around small form factor PCs isn't about aesthetics. It's about doing more with less — and the 7050 SFF is proof that enterprise-grade hardware doesn't need enterprise-grade space.

🎯
Desk-friendly footprint

At roughly the size of a hardback novel, the 7050 SFF fits anywhere. Wall-mounted, under-monitor, in a media cabinet. Enterprise hardware, consumer-sized.

📱
Silent running

Lower TDP components and efficient airflow mean this runs quieter than most gaming rigs at idle. Perfect for a bedroom lab or always-on home server use.

💷
Honest value

No overpriced RTX branding, no RGB surcharges. You get real enterprise engineering — Q270 chipset, Intel NIC, TPM 2.0 — at a fraction of the retail cost.

⚡
Modular & upgradeable

Dell's tool-free chassis means RAM, storage, and GPU swaps take minutes, not hours. The 7050 SFF was designed to be maintained. That's not common anymore.
